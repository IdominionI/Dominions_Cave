Understanding the Universe

Introduction

Within this document is an attempt to give an alternative approach of thinking and process of investigation to studying the universe so as to gain a more possible truth of understanding and knowledge of the inner workings and structure of the physical universe that the human mind observes and exists within. What is not attempted is to propose a replacement of current theory, but to express that the current thought process and accepted conventions of physics and mathematical tools and theories used to create the models of the universe may need revision and consideration of a different approach that can lead to a more clearer understanding of the universe that then can be used to modify and enhance an understanding of the universe.

By taking a different look and attempting to build from the ground up with different definitions of the fundamentals of the observed phenomenon of the physical universe that does not conflict with theory, a different perspective of what constitutes the fundamental make up and processes of the physical universe can be derived, leading to a greater approach to understanding and comprehension of what is observed.

This document first tackles the most fundamental concepts of the universe that cannot be expressed in mathematical form to be understood, but need to taken from a different angle so as to form the basis of approach from a more holistic and qualitative definition that can be applied universally. It could be considered that there is a kind of philosophical approach being applied.

Such fundamental concepts that forms the basis of all of physics such as space, time and energy are tackled first to be given what may be different definitions, interpretations and explanations as to what they are and how they fit in with the universe. These will not break with current physics, but to offer an alternative perspective of what they may be.

As this document progresses, other interpretations and explanations of physical phenomenon will be presented with arguments that may involve some mathematical treatment to help explain such an argument. But as has been discovered, many a consideration has already been presented in the past that correlates with what is proposed in this document by past minds that have put forward the same arguments with greater detail, treatment and explanation. It is comforting to find that when one comes up with what one considers as an original thought, or approach of thinking, to then find out that others have already considered such, to then know if one is on the right or wrong track.

So it can be forgiven if there are such arguments that have been proposed here that have already been documented and better presented, but not known by the author.

What is important is to consider that the current understanding of the universe as proposed and modelled by physicists can be flawed by a following bias of conventional thought. This is not uncommon in history. Many a greater understanding and modelling of the physical universe, and other scientific disciplines have been thawted by conventional authorities chained down by not accepting new or different trains of thinking. This has either been because of personal traits related to ego, reputation, status and belief systems, or by institutions unable to accept alternative positions for the same and other reasons. But this is not all together a bad thing. To change the thought processes and acceptance of new theories of understanding require strong arguments with evidence to back them up.

This document is not an attempt to change or challenge such current conventions or authorities of physics, just to offer a different approach to thinking to gain a greater understanding of the universe and its workings. The author hopes this document will be helpful in that respect.

This is a work in progress and is a draft that may change as new drafts and discoveries. Some mistakes may be found and will later be corrected as with this introduction.
