MATHEMATICS

Mathematics is a fundamental tool that is used to model the physics of the universe by creating theories of mathematical relationships between certain physical characteristics of matter that is based upon observation and measurement. These mathematical relationships are then used to build mathematical models of physical behaviour which are then used to predict results or phenomena that can be tested to see if the theory holds out. When test results equate with predictions, then the theory, or at least the mathematics of that theory is said to be correct.

Despite all the successes of using mathematics to explain and predict, mathematics may have some limits and misconceptions of its use in modelling the universe, or indeed if some of the mathematics used have certain flaws that may work in one situation, are limited or invalid in others. PHYSICAL CONSTANTS

In all of the theories of physics based upon experiment and observation of results, relationships between certain physical properties or traits are quantities of proportionality. To get one physical property to equate with another such as forces in the theory of gravity or electromagnetism equating to a measured charge of a field, one or more constant of proportionality is introduced. It is proposed that these constants have a large bearing and influence on the true nature of understanding that needs to be achieved to understand the universe and its workings. The relationships of electromagnetism and Newtonian gravity have a very similar resemblance to each other in structure. This ,may also suggest a relationship between the constants G for gravity and permittivity, ϵ0 for electric fields and permeability μ0 for magnetic fields. Indeed such a relation ship is given with the speed of light being that possible connection.

Other constants used in physics may have no real physical meaning at all as they may be a derivation of the mathematics that a model needed to fit in with observation, and is thus limited to only ideal circumstances. Thus when finding and using constants in physics, one needs to be aware of the context and validity of use of such a constant, and if it is universal such as the speed of light, or just a calculation tool as in the ideal gas equation PV=nRT.
