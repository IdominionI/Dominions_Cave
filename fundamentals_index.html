Time is the progression of a physical entity, or system of entities transitioning from one physical state A to another physical state B, and that this transition has a consistent and discrete period of delay to do so with differing rates of transitioning between different physical states of transition of the same or different entities.

Energy is the physical substrate by means of interaction or process that causes an entity, or system of entities to change from one physical state A to another physical state B. On the most fundamental level, it are the progenitor causes of the forces of electromagnetism and gravity that are defined as energy. That is electromagnetic and gravitational fields.

Since time and energy have as their basis of definition a physical transition of an an entity from one physical state A to another physical state B, time and energy should thus have a relationship to each other.

MATHEMATICS

Mathematics is a fundamental tool that is used to model the physics of the universe by creating theories of mathematical relationships between certain physical characteristics of matter that is based upon observation and measurement. These mathematical relationships are then used to build mathematical models of physical behaviour which are then used to predict results or phenomena that can be tested to see if the theory holds out. When test results equate with predictions, then the theory, or at least the mathematics of that theory is said to be correct.

Despite all the successes of using mathematics to explain and predict, mathematics may have some limits and misconceptions of its use in modelling the universe, or indeed if some of the mathematics used have certain flaws that may work in one situation, are limited or invalid in others. It is proposed that these constants have a large bearing and influence on the true nature of understanding that needs to be achieved to understand the universe and its workings. Other constants used in physics may have no real physical meaning at all as they may be a derivation of the mathematics that a model needed to fit in with observation, and is thus limited to only ideal circumstances.
